CONSTRUCTION COST AUDITING

A construction cost audit can result in savings, while assisting owners and developers in meeting stakeholder requirements. Whether the owner/developer has entered into a cost plus fee contract or a lump sum/fixed price contract, the risk of overcharges is the same. Most overcharges are due to misunderstandings between the owner/developer and general contractor, oversights by supervisors, and misinterpretations of the contract due to the significant volume of documents involved in the management and administration of a construction project.

About Me and Retrieval

While I was dreaming to be an Individual practitioner, It came to me so many areas one on another to establish a Professional PRACTICE and finally I decided in linking few key areas which were encountered, after reviewing the knowledge what I have gained though last 20 years and by surveying a particular room for me to render my service arisen out of my interests and competencies. Key words which I linked together for an appropriate practice are; COST, FINAL ACCOUNTS, LESSON - LEARNED PRACICES, NON VALUE ADDING ACTVITIES, VALUE FOR MONEY. Basically, the Lesson Learned Practices was the area of my MSc (Project Management) Thesis and Non value adding activities was the research problem for my MPhil Research where the approach was Lean Implementation. All other key words are within the zone of QS. Further, being an employee of serveral client organizations, working with different grades of contractors, I revealed that examination of Final Accounts by an independent party has become so vital for many reasons.
